# Microbial Hydrogen Production Performance Metrics

This reference provides typical hydrogen production rates (HPR) and yields (HY) for key species and substrates.

## High-Rate H2 Producers (Batch Fermentation)

| Species | Primary Pathway | Substrate | Temp (°C) | pH | H2 Yield (mol H2/mol substrate) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| *Clostridium butyricum* | [FeFe]-Hyd | Glucose | 37 | 6.0 | 2.1 - 2.4 |
| *Clostridium beijerinckii* | [FeFe]-Hyd | Glucose | 35 | 6.5 | 1.8 - 2.0 |
| *Escherichia coli* | FHL (Formate) | Glucose | 37 | 5.5 | 0.8 - 1.2 |
| *Thermotoga maritima* | [FeFe] (Hyperthermophile) | Starch | 80 | 7.0 | 3.5 - 4.0 |
| *Rhodobacter sphaeroides* | Nitrogenase (Photo) | Lactate | 30 | 7.5 | 4.5 - 6.0 |

## Typical Inhibitory Concentrations
- **Oxygen (O2):** Most [FeFe] and [NiFe] are highly sensitive (< 1 ppm).
- **Ammonium (NH4+):** Inhibits Nitrogenase-based H2 production.
- **Nitrate (NO3-):** Competes for electrons.

## Common Substrate-Yield Guidelines
- **Theoretical Limit (Thauer Limit):** 4 mol H2/mol Glucose (via acetate).
- **Practical Batch Fermentation:** 1.5 - 2.5 mol H2/mol Glucose.
- **Combined Photo-Fermentation:** Can reach 8-10 mol H2/mol Glucose.
