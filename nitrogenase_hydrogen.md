# Nitrogenase-Linked Hydrogen Production

This reference documents the specific hydrogen-producing properties of nitrogenases, which are NOT included in the HydDB classification.

## The Obligate H2 Co-production

The reduction of N2 to NH3 by Nitrogenase is inherently coupled with H2 evolution.

**Stoichiometry:**
N2 + 8H+ + 8e- + 16MgATP → 2NH3 + H2 + 16MgADP + 16Pi

**Efficiency Note:**
- At least 25% of the electron flux is lost as H2 under physiological conditions.
- Under N2-deficient conditions (Ar environment), 100% of the electrons can be diverted to H2 production.

## Comparison with Hydrogenases

| Feature | Nitrogenase | [FeFe]-Hydrogenase |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **ATP Requirement** | High (16 ATP/N2) | Low (None) |
| **H2 Yield** | Moderate | Very High |
| **Inhibition** | O2, CO | O2 |
| **Main Advantage** | Multi-functional (N-fix + H2) | Specialized for high-rate H2 |
